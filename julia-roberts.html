Who is Julia Roberts?

Julia Roberts is a critically-acclaimed American actress, voiceover artist, and producer who has won 3 Golden Globe Awards from 8 nominations and has been nominated for 4 Academy Awards for her film acting, winning the Academy Award for “Best Actress” for playing the titular character in the biographical legal drama film Erin Brockovich (2000). She is also known for her work in movies like Eat Pray Love, Pretty Woman, Notting Hill, Runaway Bride, Mona Lisa Smile, Ocean’s Twelve, Charlie Wilson’s War, Steel Magnolias, August: Osage County, My Best Friend’s Wedding, and Mirror, Mirror. Roberts is considered one of the most bankable actresses in Hollywood and was the highest-paid actress in the world throughout most of the 1990s and in the first half of the 2000s. Moreover, she has also been named the most beautiful woman in the world by People magazine a record five times.

Julia Roberts Career 

My apologies for any confusion. Let’s dive into Julia Roberts’ journey in the entertainment world. She kicked off her big-screen career with “Satisfaction,” starring alongside Liam Neeson and Justine Bateman. There was also a family collaboration when she worked with her brother Eric Anthony Roberts in the film “Blood Red” in 1987, although it didn’t hit theaters until 1989.

Her small-screen debut happened on the series “Crime Story,” where she portrayed a teenage assault victim in an episode titled “The Survivor” on February 13, 1987. But the real breakthrough came with her stellar performance in the indie film “Mystic Pizza” in 1988. The same year, she graced the fourth-season finale of “Miami Vice.”

The spotlight truly found her in 1989 when she took on the role of a young woman dealing with diabetes in “Steel Magnolias.” This performance earned her two Academy Award nominations for “Best Supporting Actress” and a Golden Globe Award for “Best Supporting Actress.”

Then came the game-changer in 1990 – “Pretty Woman.” Julia starred alongside Richard Gere in this Cinderella-esque tale, playing a confident and kind-hearted independent prostitute. This movie catapulted her to international stardom, and she shared the screen with other Hollywood heavyweights like Michelle Pfeiffer, Molly Ringwald, Meg Ryan, Jennifer Jason Leigh, Karen Allen, and Daryl Hannah in the popular film “Steel Magnolias.”

Her role in “Pretty Woman” not only earned her a second Oscar nomination for Best Actress but also snagged her a second Golden Globe Award for Motion Picture Best Actress. The film itself became the highest-grossing romantic comedy in the U.S., raking in over $463.4 million worldwide. Julia Roberts’ journey in the entertainment industry is a tale of versatility, talent, and well-deserved success.

Julia Roberts Husband/Boyfriend

Several well-known celebrities, such as Jason Patric, Liam Neeson, Kiefer Sutherland, Dylan McDermott, and Matthew Perry, were close friends of Julia Roberts. Her engagement to Sutherland ended only three days before to their planned nuptials.

Julia and singer Lyle Lovett were married on June 25, 1993, in a lovely ceremony held at St. James Lutheran Church in Marion, Indiana. But they reached a turning point in their relationship, which resulted in their breakup in March 1995. After that, she had a love connection with actor Benjamin Bratt from 1998 to 2001, but it did not work out.

In a twist of fate, Julia unexpectedly met cinematographer Daniel Moder on the set of her film “The Mexican.” A year later, on July 4, 2002, the two tied the knot at her farm in Taos, New Mexico. The couple is now blessed with three children, including sweet twins born in 2004 (a daughter and a son) and another adorable child born in 2007. Julia Roberts’ journey through relationships has had its ups and downs, but it ultimately led her to a loving family life with Daniel Moder and their precious children.
