Ama Qamata (born September 2, 1998) is a South African Actress and Social Media Personality from Cape Town. She garnered immense success after starring in the South African TV series “Gomora”.

The series was produced by Seriti Films. The show came out on March 30, 2020. As per the source, Ama acted for one of the main casts as Buhle Ndaba who is the daughter of Thati Ndaba (performed by Katlego Danke).

Zeno Pieterson worked as a director of the series. Some of the other main casts of Gomora are Connie Chiume (as Mam’Sonto), Thembi Seete (as Gladys Dlamini), Zolisa Xaluva (as Melusi Dlamini), Ntobeko Sishi (as Ntokozo Dlamini), etc.

On May 20, 2020, she rose to international fame after her new show “Blood & Water” was released. It is a South African Netflix original series directed by Daryne Joshua, Nosipho Dumisa, and Travis Taute.

Qamata performed for a lead character named Puleng Khumal along with Khosi Ngema as Fikile Bhele and Gail Mabalane as Thandeka Khumalo. The actress worked with actors like Thabang Molaba (as Karabo Molapo ‘KB’), Dillon Windvogel (as Wade Daniels), Ryle De Morny (as Chad Morgan), Arno Greeff (as Chris Ackerman), Greteli Fincham (as Reece Van Rensburg), Getmore Sithole (as Julius Khumalo), etc.
